Projects

This is my most recent project I've worked with a team of other programmers. This was for a hackathon called CruzHacks at UC Santa Cruz. This was my second hackathon at this point and I was still learning some of the fundamentals of coding. For this competition, my team and I wanted to code an Amazon Alexa just because it seemed like an interesting product to work with. We wanted to make something with Alexa that would emphasize communicating to you versus a feedback response. Our initial product idea was to use an accelerometer and connect it with Alexa so it would be able to detect and send notifications of an earthquake. After extensive research and having the Amazon Alexa judge tell us that this product would be impossible. It was disheartening to hear the impossibility of our idea but he also mentioned how it's impossible to send notifications to itself. My team and I knew that he was wrong about that after finding API's online. We decided to restructure our product to be a notification system in case a relative was in an accident. We had Alexa compile information from USGS to check for fire information and compared with a database of contacts to see if an friend/relative was near a fire and it would notify you. We got our product working and used an earthquake simulator to represent damage and created a working proof of concept. During this project, I learned a lot of JSON and started working with databases with website development. It was an extremely fun project to work on and I'm learning more about JSON mainly because of this project. I was really proud of my team for getting 2nd place in the Amazon Alexa category overall.
